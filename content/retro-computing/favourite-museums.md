## Computing

### Bletchley Park
[Bletchley Park](https://bletchleypark.org.uk/) - The historic site of World War II codebreaking and the birthplace of modern computing. Located in Milton Keynes, UK.

### The National Museum of Computing
[TNMOC](https://www.tnmoc.org/) - Located on the Bletchley Park estate, this museum houses the world's largest collection of functional historic computers, including the rebuilt Colossus and the Harwell Dekatron (WITCH).

### Centre for Computing History
[Centre for Computing History](https://www.computinghistory.org.uk/) - Based in Cambridge, this museum focuses on the personal computing revolution and features a massive collection of vintage consoles and computers.

## Science

### Natural History Museum
[Natural History Museum](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/) - World-renowned museum of natural history in South Kensington, London. Home to dinosaur fossils, earth sciences, and vast biology collections.

### British Museum
[British Museum](https://www.britishmuseum.org/) - A world-famous museum in Bloomsbury, London, dedicated to human history, art, and culture. Its collection covers over two million years of human history.

### Victoria and Albert Museum
[Victoria and Albert Museum](https://www.vam.ac.uk/) - The world's largest museum of decorative arts and design, located in South Kensington, London. It houses over 2.3 million objects spanning 5,000 years of human creativity.

### Imperial War Museum
[Imperial War Museum](https://www.iwm.org.uk/) - Documenting the experience of modern warfare and conflict, with exhibits covering from the First World War to the present day. Located in Lambeth, London.

### Science Museum
[Science Museum](https://www.sciencemuseum.org.uk/) - London's national museum of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics in South Kensington. Features historic aircraft, space exploration exhibits, and significant computing history.

### Royal Observatory Greenwich
[Royal Observatory](https://www.rmg.co.uk/royal-observatory) - The home of Greenwich Mean Time (GMT) and the Prime Meridian. Features historic astronomical instruments and the John Harrison sea clocks.

## Art Galleries

### National Gallery
[National Gallery](https://www.nationalgallery.org.uk/) - London's premier public art gallery located in Trafalgar Square. Houses one of the greatest collections of European paintings in the world, from the 13th to the 19th centuries.
